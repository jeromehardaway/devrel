Hi, My Name Is Jerome Hardaway. Its great to e-meet you.

I am a hopeful Developer Advocate for Quicken Loans. After leaving the Air Force at the height of the recession, I retooled myself to become a programmer. While seeing others struggle post-service, I thought to myself, " If it worked for me, it would work for them." So I started teaching other veterans remotely how to program. To date, I have helped over 250 veterans connect and learn this crazy alchemy where we tell the computer what to do called web development. While holding down a job and performing the duties assigned to me, I have been a fierce advocate for training veterans for today's skills NOW , not later, while leading from the front and pushing troops to become better prepared for the current workforce by mastering the tools of the digital age.

I have performed talks at conferences like OSCON, had the opportunity to write for Stack Overflow, and podcasted as a host on Ruby Rogues. I have showcased the ability to not only educate but entertain while bringing a level of realism into the industry that only one who has seen the world on its worst days and still found the silver lining.

Front End Architecture, CSS, and Design Systems make me happy. I hope in the future to lead teams on building front ends that make other programmers envious while helping the business with its goals. The web is a little boring with the coolest toys saved for portfolios. I believe that the world is ready for some spice on the internet, and I hope to one day be the one able to serve it to them without constraints. I find CSS therapeutic, and the idea of making it scalable for large teams excites me.
